Counseling and Psychological Services

What is CAPS?

CAPS is committed to creating an environment based on our values of multicultural, multi-disciplinary, and multi-theoretical practices that allow our diverse student body to access care, receive high-quality services, and take positive pathways to mental health. They are happy to offer both in-person and virtual services.

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) provides a range of clinical, preventative, and training services consistent with the practices and standards of a nationally accredited university counseling center. CAPS is committed to creating a safe and supportive environment. Our team of experienced counselors helps address a variety of student concerns, ranging from stress and anxiety to identity-related issues, promoting overall mental well-being.

What services are available to students?

CAPS covers a wide range of services for students, including individual counseling, group therapy, crisis support, and wellness coaching. These resources are designed to help students navigate academic stress, personal challenges, and mental health concerns. Whether you're looking for one-on-one support, peer-led workshops, or self-guided wellness tools, CAPS offers options tailored to different needs. View this page to understand more about the scope of services CAPS provides.

View this page to understand more about the scope of services CAPS provides. The following services are also available to students:

  • Mental Health Support Options & Sexualized Violence Resources

    CAPS offers a variety of mental health support services, including one-on-one counseling, group therapy, and crisis intervention. Additionally, CAPS provides specialized resources for survivors of sexualized violence, helping students navigate recovery with confidential support and advocacy.

  • First Year Guide

    Adjusting to college can be overwhelming, and the First Year Guide provides tailored support to help new students transition successfully. The guide covers strategies for stress management, academic balance, and maintaining mental well-being in the first year at Michigan.

  • Support for Students Impacted by Campus Climate Concerns

    CAPS is dedicated to fostering an inclusive and supportive campus environment. If you're affected by campus climate issues, such as discrimination, bias incidents, or social tensions, CAPS offers safe spaces, support groups, and individual counseling to help students process their experiences and develop coping strategies.

How do I book an appointment?

Scheduling an appointment with CAPS is simple and can be done online or by phone. Visit this page to find step-by-step instructions on how to book an appointment, whether you’re seeking individual counseling, group therapy, or wellness coaching. You’ll also find information on what to expect during your first visit and any necessary forms to complete beforehand.

If you have an urgent mental health concern or are experiencing a crisis, immediate support is available. Visit this page to learn about crisis intervention services, same-day urgent care options, and 24/7 support resources.

Get Involved with CAPS

CAPS encourages students to get involved in their community well-being initiatives. Students can participate in peer support programs and contribute to creating a well-rounded, empathetic campus culture. Consider joining the CAPS Ambassador program to advocate for mental health awareness across campus.
