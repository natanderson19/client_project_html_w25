Maize & Blue Cupboard

What is the Maize & Blue Cupboard?

College students and staff are experiencing food insecurity at alarming rates. The Maize and Blue Cupboard is here to provide an immediate and comprehensive response for the U-M community. By offering resources, educational opportunities, compassionate support, and more, they help students develop the skills to make informed decisions. The Cupboard not only offers shelf-stable and perishable food options but also supplies personal care items, kitchen utensils, and other essentials. Their goal is to create a welcoming and non-judgmental environment where all students feel supported and empowered.

Who can access the Maize & Blue Cupboard?

Anyone with a valid U-M ID! Access to the resources at MBC is available to all students, staff, and faculty, ensuring no one goes hungry. Whether you're in need of a few groceries to get by or require ongoing assistance, the Maize & Blue Cupboard is here to help without the need for income verification.

How Does it Work?

Visiting the Maize & Blue Cupboard is simple and discreet. Just bring your M-Card and a reusable bag if possible. You'll check in upon arrival, then browse the shelves to select the items you need. Volunteers and staff are available to guide and assist during your visit, making sure you have all the necessary information about available products and any special distributions or events.

Where is the Maize & Blue Cupboard located?

Located in the basement of the Betsy Barbour Residence Hall, enter via the Maynard entrance only to ensure privacy for residents. Contact the MBC if you need ramp or elevator access.
Address: 420 S State St Ann Arbor, MI 48109
Phone: 734-936-2794

How Can I Help?

If you're interested in supporting the Maize & Blue Cupboard, there are several ways to get involved. You can volunteer your time to assist with restocking shelves, help during operating hours, or participate in educational workshops. Additionally, donations of food items, personal care products, or monetary gifts are always appreciated. Every little bit helps in making the Cupboard a reliable resource for those in need.
